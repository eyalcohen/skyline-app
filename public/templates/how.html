Interactive Chart Features

Zoom: Zoom into or out of the graph using the same gestures as an online map, or using the zoom wheel on your mouse.

Pan: Click and hold anywhere in the chart and move it to the left to pan forward in time, or move the chart to the right to pan backward in time. You can also enter times and dates directly into the time entry box to quickly narrow down to the time segment you care about.

Overview Plot: The graph running along the bottom of the page gives you an overview of the full span of the datasets you are currently viewing. Click and highlight a segment of that overview to zoom into that segment of time.

Adding and Removing Data to/from Charts

Search: The easiest way to find and add data to a chart is by using the search bar. Search for datasets by typing keywords into the search bar and Skyline will list all related datasets and channels. Selecting a result and Skyline takes you straight to a graph of what you picked.

Viewing/Hiding Channels: Once a dataset is added to a chart, you can mouse over the name of the dataset and a list of all the channels that are part of that dataset will become visible. Click on any channel name to make it visible on the chart. Click the channel name again to hide the channel.

Adding a Dataset: Skyline gives you the power to instantly layer channels from multiple datasets onto the same chart. To add a dataset, use the Search bar and whatever dataset you select will be added to the chart. You can also add a dataset using the "Add Dataset[c]" button which allows you to search for data, upload new data, or browse data you have previously uploaded.

Chart Formatting

Axis Scaling: As you interact with your chart, Skyline automatically scales the axis to ensure that all the data remains visible on the screen at the highest resolution possible for the channels being viewed.

Changing Axes: If you are ever looking at two channels with vastly different scales, it might be helpful to rescale the vertical axis of one of those channels. To toggle the axis for a specific channel, click on the arrow next to the channel name and Skyline will rescale that axis and shift that channel to the rescaled axis.

Chart Resolution: As you zoom out of high-resolution data, Skyline will automatically show average data points to make the chart more readable. Skyline will also show high-lighted bands around the averaged data in order to indicate max/min values in the raw data underlying the data being displayed.

Changing Line Formats: Hover your mouse over a channel name to make the line formatting window appear. In the formatting window, you can change the plot type (scatter plot, line graph, or area graph), interpolation method (linear or square wave), and line thickness and color.

Sharing and Collaboration

Skyline lets you save, share, and collaborate on data with the ease of a social network.

Dataset Details: Clicking the details button displays a sidebar showing detailed information about what you are viewing. This will include a description, source information, number of channels, and a list of tags associated with the dataset or view that you are viewing.

What is a View?: A "View" in Skyline is a snapshot of your chart exactly as you are viewing it. A saved View remembers the combination of datasets and channels that you are viewing along with the specific time segment visible on the screen and the formatting you used for each plot. Clicking on a View will take you directly back to the chart that you were viewing without you having to hunt for data in any way.

Saving a View: While working with a chart, click the "Save" icon in the upper right corner to Save a "View". Skyline will ask for a title, description, and a privacy setting for the view. Once your view is saved, it will appear in your news feed. If you save the View as "Public", it will also appear in the news feed of your followers and in Skyline search results. (Note that Views can only be saved as "Public" if all datasets within the View are Public.

Commenting on Data: Skyline makes it easy to comment and collaborate on data. You can comment on any dataset or view in your Feed or in Dataset Details sidebar. Skyline also allows you to attach a note directly onto a chart. Click and drag the Note button on the bottom of the graph to highlight a segment of data and create a comment straight onto the chart.

Follow Users: Want to keep track of what your friends are doing on Skyline? Search for their name in order to get to their Profile page. Then click "Follow". Now, anytime they upload a dataset, create a view, or make comment, it will show up in your Feed.

My Feed: Your Feed is your homebase on Skyline. In addition to showing you everything you have been doing, your feed also shows you datasets, views, and comments from other Skyline users that you follow. You can access your Feed by clicking "My Feed" or by clicking the SkylineBeta icon from any page.

Public Data: Skyline's library of public data is continuously growing. Every day, our own data researchers along with our community of users are adding public data sets which you can visualize for free.

When you export the data, only the data from the current view is included in the file. Once you've downloaded the data, you can use it to tell your story.

With Skyline, you can visualize and select the data you want before you download a huge dataset. This helps eliminate the hassle of poring through a huge dataset trying to find the information you want.
